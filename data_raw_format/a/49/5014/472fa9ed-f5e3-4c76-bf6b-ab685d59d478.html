Research

Research Interests

Early America
social history of seafaring
work and economic culture

Research

I am currently working on a study of the borrowing and lending habits of Matthew Patten, a New England farmer of the eighteenth century.  This work will use his personal diary and accounts to examine the distinction between commercial and neighborly obligations in the pre-industrial world. By uncovering his rules of repayment, I hope to add to our understanding of early modern economic culture.

Books

Citation Book/Journal Cover
D. Vickers. A Companion to Colonial America. Oxford: Wiley-Blackwell, 2008.

A Companion to Colonial America consists of twenty-three original essays by expert historians on the key issues and topics in American colonial history. Each essay surveys the scholarship and prevailing interpretations in these key areas, discussing the differing arguments and assessing their merits. Coverage includes politics, religion, migration, gender, ecology, and many others.
