Biography






This paragraph is not available in English, therefore the Swedish version is shown.


Present responsibilities 
 

Acting head of Department of Business Studies at Uppsala University.
 

Research
 

Accounting, management accounting and management control systems.
 

Present research project
 

Undergoing changes
 

Academic education
 

• Ekonomie doktor. PhD, Doctorate degree in management accounting at Mälardalen University, 2010
 

• Ekonomie magister. MSc, Master degree in management accounting at Mälardalen University, 2001
 

• Ekonomexamen. BA, Bachelor degree in accounting and organization at Uppsala University, 1985
 

Languages
 

Swedish and English

Lecturing assignments
 

Guest lecture assignments
 

Annually since 2002, guest lecturer at University of Applied Science Vorarlberg, Austria. www.fhv.at
 

Assistant professor at the accounting section at Stockholm Business School, Stockholm university (part-time, september 2014 - january 2015).
 

 
 

CV
 

• As of August 2008: Senior lecturer and assistant professor (PhD) in accounting and mangement accounting at the Department of Business studies, Uppsala University
 

• 1992 - 2008: Lecturer and tutor in accounting and management control at Mälardalen University College. Also acting as director of studies in business administration, responsible for aspects of international activities at the School of Business, facultymember at the department board at School of Business, facultymember at board of Mälardalen University, head of undergraduate education for business students and vice head of department (School of Business)
 

• 1991 - 1992: Financial controller and internal auditor at ICA, the largest food retail chain stores in Sweden
 

• 1989 - 1990: CFO-assistant at ICA Köpmannatjänst, the largest food retail chain stores in Sweden
 

• 1986 - 1989: Financial and management accountant at a Swedish public radio station
 

• 1980 - 1985: Worked internationally for different Swedish tour operators including lengthy periods in Austria, Great Britain and Greece
