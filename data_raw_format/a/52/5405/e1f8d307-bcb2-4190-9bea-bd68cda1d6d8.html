Burwood Corporate Centre

Overview

Located at the Melbourne Burwood Campus, the Burwood Corporate Centre (BCC) is Deakin’s largest corporate centre. Featuring 10 stylish, technology-rich rooms and two lounges, the centre hosts meetings and events for up to 210 people. 

BCC also offers hassle-free catering for events – from a light morning tea or boardroom lunches to large cocktail parties.

Getting there

Regional train

Plan your journey on the Public Transport Victoria (PTV) website

Train

From Melbourne CBD it takes about an hour to get to Deakin’s Burwood Campus using combinations of train, tram and bus.

Plan your journey on the Public Transport Victoria (PTV) website

Tram

The most direct journey is by tram. Take the 75 tram to Stop 63 (Deakin University/Burwood Highway).

Plan your journey on the Public Transport Victoria (PTV) website

Myki

To use trams, trains and buses around Melbourne you’ll need to buy a Myki card. 

Myki cards are very easy to use, just top up credit or buy a pass at a shop or station, then touch-on once you’ve boarded the tram or bus – or touch-on and off to get through the barriers at train stations.

Visit the Public Transport Victoria (PTV) Myki site

Cycle

The most direct cycle route to the Burwood Campus from Melbourne CBD is around 15km and takes about an hour (there are a couple of quite steep hills to contend with). 

There are plenty of bike trails around the campus so commuting on your bike is definitely an option.

On campus you’ll find plenty of showers, change rooms, bike racks, lockers and a secure bicycle cage.

Find out about local cycling news, resources and events

Car

The Burwood Campus is a 25-minute drive from Melbourne CBD, depending on traffic.

  • From Melbourne CBD join the Monash Freeway (M1)
  • Take the Burke Road exit
  • Turn right at Toorak Road and follow it for about 10 minutes
  • Toorak Road becomes Burwood Highway and you’ll see Deakin University on the left

Car pooling

If you bring someone else with you in your car, you can use the car pooling car park. 

We actually provide a free carpool-matching service to students and staff to help you find someone to carpool with.

Parking

Paid parking is available on campus at a number of different car parks. You can pay for short-term parking using the CellOPark app, and students and staff can register for long-term parking via vPermit. 

Get more details on parking and parking permits
