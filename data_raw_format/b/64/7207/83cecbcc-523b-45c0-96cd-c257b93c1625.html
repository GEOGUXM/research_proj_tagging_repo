Doctor Norm Zabriskie received his medical degree from the University of Utah School of Medicine and completed his ophthalmology residency at the University of Iowa. Doctor Zabriskie then went on to complete a fellowship in glaucoma at the University of Iowa, followed by an additional fellowship in glaucoma at the University of Utah. Dr. Zabriskie specializes in all medical and surgical treatments for glaucoma in both adults and children. He also performs cataract surgery and other anterior segment procedures.

Doctor Zabriskie is Associate Professor of Ophthalmology and Visual Sciences, Vice Chair and Medical Director of Clinical Services, and Director of Clinical Operations for the Moran Eye Center at the University of Utah. Doctor Zabriskie is certified by the American Board of Ophthalmology. He is a member of the American Academy of Ophthalmology, American Glaucoma Society, and the Utah Ophthalmological Society. In addition, he received the Achievement Award from the American Academy of Ophthalmology in 2005 and is a past president of the Utah Ophthalmologic Society. Dr. Zabriskie lectures both regionally and nationally on multiple glaucoma topics and has been listed in the Best Doctors in America since 2005.
