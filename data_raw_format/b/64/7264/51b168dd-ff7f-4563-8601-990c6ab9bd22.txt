Richard Bayliss
Direct Contact Details:
Tel:
Email: Richard.Bayliss@le.ac.uk
  Career
1994-1997         BA(hons) 1st class in Natural Sciences, Trinity College, University of Cambridge
1997-2000         PhD studies in the laboratory of Murray Stewart at the MRC Laboratory of Molecular Biology, Cambridge. Thesis title: Role of FxFG Nucleoporins in nuclear trafficking.
2000-2001         MRC Postdoctoral Fellow and Trinity College Research Fellow at the MRC Laboratory of Molecular Biology, Cambridge.
2001-2003         EMBO Long Term Fellow in the laboratory of Elena Conti at the European Molecular Biology Laboratory (EMBL), Heidelberg, Germany.
2003-2005         Wellcome Trust Postdoctoral Fellow in the laboratory of Gabriel Waksman, Birkbeck College, University of London
2006-2011         Royal Society Research Fellow & Career Development Faculty at the Institute of Cancer Research, London
2011- 2014                Reader and Royal Society Research Fellow in the Department of Biochemistry, University of Leicester
2014-                  Professor in the Department of Biochemistry, University of Leicester
  Research Interests
  Molecular mechanisms of mitosis and cell division
Structure-based drug design
Ciliopathies
Precision medicine
