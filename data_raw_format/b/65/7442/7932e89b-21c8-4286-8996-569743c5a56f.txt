Project(s)

Microbiology of extremely acidic terrestrial volcanic ecosystems
Short description project(s)

Despite having major impact on geochemical cycles and greenhouse gas emissions the microbial collective of volcanic ecosystems has hardly been explored nor exploited. Therefore, the aim of my project is to obtain a fundamental understanding of the microbial ecology of extremely acid terrestrial mud volcanoes with special emphasis on the microbial diversity, cellular structure, metabolic potential, and the molecular response to changing environmental conditions of microorganisms involved in the key elemental cycles of sulfur, methane and nitrogen. An amplicon PCR sequencing approach targeting functional genes of different trophic groups will be followed. When starting this approach from both extracted DNA and mRNA, the results will show presence, biodiversity gene expression of the different trophic types of microorganisms of the different nutrient cycles. The molecular approach will be complemented by activity measurements and enrichment cultures under controlled conditions in batch and continuous bioreactors.
