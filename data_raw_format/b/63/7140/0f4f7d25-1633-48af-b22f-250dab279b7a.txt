Samantha Hulme
Adjunct professor
Samantha Hulme Faculty of Law
University of Victoria
PO Box 1700, STN CSC
Victoria, BC   V8W 2Y2
Map 
Samantha Hulme articled with the BC Ministry of Attorney General and has worked as Crown Counsel throughout the Lower Mainland and most recently, Victoria, for 21 years. As trial counsel Samantha prosecuted the typical array of Criminal Code matters and worked as Administrative Crown Counsel in Maple Ridge and Port Coquitlam.
She has spent the last 6 years with the Criminal Justice Branch (CBJ) HQ working in criminal law and justice system reform and policy development. She attends national criminal law reform discussions on behalf of the BC Ministry of Justice, and acts as the backup CJB Communications (media) Counsel.
In 2011 she received the Ministry of Attorney General Exemplary Service Award for Stakeholder Relations. She has served as President of the New Westminster Bar Association and Chair of the New Westminster CBA Criminal Subsection. She served as Westminster County’s Governor on Board of the Law Foundation of BC for 6 years, and in that capacity was the Liaison Governor for the Pro Bono Students Canada program at UVIC and UBC. Most recently Samantha has been designated as the recipient of the Federal-Provincial-Territorial Heads of Prosecutions Committee Humanitarian Award for 2013 recognizing her service to the profession, and to the local and international community.
Samantha co taught Criminal Procedure with defence counsel Martin Allen for the Spring 2013 session and is happy to be back sparring with Martin for the Spring 2014 term.
