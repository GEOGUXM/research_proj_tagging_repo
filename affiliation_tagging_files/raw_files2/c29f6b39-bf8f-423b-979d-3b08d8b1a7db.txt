AREAS OF RESEARCH
Constitutional Law, Executive Branch Legal Interpretation, Habeas Corpus and Executive Detention, Separation of Powers
Trevor Morrison is dean and also the Eric M. and Laurie B. Roth Professor of Law at NYU School of Law. Before coming to NYU, he was on the faculties of Cornell Law School (2003-08) and Columbia Law School (2008-13). In 2009, Morrison served as associate counsel to President Barack Obama. FEATURED WORK
“Presidential Power, Historical Practice, And Legal Constraint”
The scope of the president’s legal authority is determined in part by historical practice. Trevor Morrison and Curtis Bradley examine how such practice-based law might work as a constraint on the presidency.
