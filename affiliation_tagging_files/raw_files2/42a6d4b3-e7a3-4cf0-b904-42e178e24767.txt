Résumé of W.H. (Wim) Bakker, MSc
In 1985 Wim Bakker received his Bachelor degree (BSc) in Electrical Engineering at the University of Twente. From 1985 to 1990 he worked as a research assistant in the Computer Vision Laboratory of the University of Twente for the Dissim project. Dissim, dis play sim ulation, was an international Esprit project funded by the European Union and involved the development of a simulator machine of modern flat-panel displays.
In 1990 he was the co-founder of the Imix company, which builds low-cost image-processing solutions for institutes and industry. In 1993 he left Imix for ITC and started working for the Image Processing Laboratory (IPL). At first as a project manager for the development of the Alexander educational software. Later, when the IPL was renamed to Remote Sensing and GIS Laboratory (RSG), as a scientific support officer.
In 2001 he moved to the scientific department of Geoinformation Processing (GIP) to hold a position as lecturer in Applied Computer Science and GIS. Within the department he is involved in the knowledge node Geodata Management and Engineering (GME).
In 2002 Wim Bakker received his Master of Science degree (MSc) in Geoinformatics at ITC. The title of his MSc thesis is "Component geoinformation services and building blocks of image processing".
Wim Bakker has worked as a lecturer in different courses at postgraduate and MSc level, has worked in research and consulting projects, and is currently involved in the inter-departmental research project "Strengthening local authorities in risk management", SLARIM, which deals with the development of a methodology for the application of Geoinformation for urban risk assessment and management.
